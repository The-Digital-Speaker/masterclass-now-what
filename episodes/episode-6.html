The Core Problem

The 5% vs. 75% Retention Gap

Traditional education — lectures, passive consumption, one-way knowledge transfer — retains approximately 5% of information. Learning-by-doing, problem-based learning, and collaborative practice retain up to 75%.

In the Intelligence Age, where the half-life of skills is shrinking from decades to years, this isn't an academic distinction — it's a strategic emergency. Organisations that still train their people through slide decks and webinars are building on sand.

Key Insight

AI can memorise everything. What AI cannot (yet) do: adapt to novel situations with emotional intelligence, build trust across cultures, ask the right questions when the answer isn't in the data. These are the skills worth investing in.

Case Study: Finland

Finland embedded media literacy and critical thinking across its entire educational curriculum — not as a separate subject, but as a lens applied to every discipline. The result: one of the world's most misinformation-resistant populations. This isn't just education reform. It's national defence.

Case Study: Khan Academy

Khan Academy demonstrated that technology can democratise learning — making world-class education available to anyone with an internet connection. AI-powered tutoring is the next frontier: personalised, adaptive, infinitely patient instruction at scale.
